# 10 — Career narrative

**What lives here.** Who the candidate is professionally and where they are going: the through-line that explains the sequence of roles, what they want next and why, the roles and companies being targeted, industries or company sizes they do and do not want, deal-breakers, how they work (ownership, first principles, collaboration style, what they are known for on a team), their self-assessed strengths and the gaps they know they have, and the explanations for anything a recruiter will notice — career gaps, a pivot, a short tenure, a step sideways.

This file drives the CV summary and every positioning decision. It is opinion and intention, not evidence — which is exactly why it is kept separate from files 2–9.

**What does not live here.** Anything checkable — facts go in files 1–9. Specific job applications and their outcomes are not tracked in `knowledge/` at all: each one lives in its own folder under `applications/`. A lesson learned from an application is only recorded here once it has become a durable statement about direction or positioning, not as a log entry.

**How to record it.** Short prose paragraphs under the subheadings below, written in the candidate's own framing and, where they said something well, their own words. Keep it current: when the target changes, rewrite rather than append.
