# 8 — Publications and talks

**What lives here.** Anything the candidate authored or presented publicly: peer-reviewed papers, preprints, patents and applications, conference or meetup talks, workshops taught, blog posts and technical write-ups, open-source documentation of note, podcasts and interviews. Per item: full title, venue or publication, date, co-authors and the candidate's position among them, link or DOI, and one line on the contribution and why it mattered.

**What does not live here.** The code behind a paper or post goes in `6-projects.md`; cross-reference rather than duplicating. Citation counts, awards or best-paper mentions also get an entry in `9-awards-and-recognition.md`.

**How to record it.** Grouped under `##` headings by kind (Papers, Patents, Talks, Writing), most recent first within each. One `-` entry per item with title, venue, date, authorship position and link. Note the audience size or reach where it is known — a talk to 400 people reads differently from a lunch-and-learn.
