# 10 — Career narrative

**What lives here.** Who the candidate is professionally and where they are going: the through-line that explains the sequence of roles; **what motivates them** — what they are optimising their career for and why the next move matters to them; their **career goals**, the roles and companies being targeted, industries or company sizes they lean toward or away from; the kinds of work that **energise** them versus the kinds that **drain** them; how they work (ownership, first principles, collaboration style, what they are known for on a team); their self-assessed strengths and the gaps they know they have; and the explanations for anything a recruiter will notice — career gaps, a pivot, a short tenure, a step sideways.

This file drives the CV summary and every positioning decision, and it is where the cover-letter angle-inference reads the candidate's motivation and fit from. It is opinion and intention, not evidence — which is exactly why it is kept separate from files 2–9.

**What does not live here.** Anything checkable — facts go in files 1–9. **Hard deal-breakers** — the concrete, non-negotiable conditions under which the candidate would decline a role (no relocation, a compensation floor, no on-call, a clearance they cannot get) — are practical constraints and live in `1-identity-and-constraints.md`, where the fit score gates on them; only *soft* aversions the candidate merely leans away from belong here, under "Industries and cultures to avoid". Specific job applications and their outcomes are not tracked in `knowledge/` at all: each one lives in its own folder under `applications/`. A lesson learned from an application is only recorded here once it has become a durable statement about direction or positioning, not as a log entry.

**How to record it.** Short prose paragraphs under the subheadings below, written in the candidate's own framing and, where they said something well, their own words. Keep it current: when the target changes, rewrite rather than append.
