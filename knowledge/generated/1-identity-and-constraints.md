# 1 — Identity and constraints

**What lives here.** Everything a recruiter needs to contact the candidate and to know whether they can legally and practically take the job: full name and any name variants, email, phone, city and country, LinkedIn, GitHub, personal site or portfolio, spoken languages with levels, work authorisation, visa status, relocation and remote willingness, and notice period or earliest start date. Also record the candidate's **hard deal-breakers** — the concrete, non-negotiable conditions under which they would decline a role however appealing it is otherwise (no relocation, remote-only, no on-call, a travel ceiling, a clearance they cannot obtain). These practical constraints, together with the location/relocation stance below, are what the fit score gates on. Also record what must *not* appear on a CV (a phone number the candidate would rather omit, an address, a photo preference).

**What does not live here.** Anything that is an accomplishment, a skill or a role — those belong in files 2–9. Career direction and what the candidate wants next belongs in `10-career-narrative.md`. So do *soft* aversions — the industries, role types or company cultures the candidate merely leans away from: those are positioning, and live under "Industries and cultures to avoid" in `10-career-narrative.md`. Only hard, practical non-negotiables belong here.

**How to record it.** One `- **Field:** value` line per fact, grouped under the subheadings below. Mark anything the candidate stated as private or omit-from-CV explicitly. This file is the sole source for the CV header block — nothing here is restated in `config.yml`, which holds switches only. A field the candidate has not given yet gets `TODO — ask candidate` rather than a guess or a placeholder.
