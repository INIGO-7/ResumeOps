# 5 — Skills inventory

**What lives here.** Every technical and professional skill the candidate can claim, with the depth and the proof behind it: languages, frameworks, libraries, databases, cloud and infrastructure, data and ML tooling, testing, methodologies, domain knowledge, and the non-technical skills that are actually demonstrable (leading a team, running a migration, client-facing work, mentoring, writing). Each skill carries how deep it goes, when it was last used in anger, and where that use is recorded.

**What does not live here.** Spoken languages go in `1-identity-and-constraints.md`.  Certifications that attest to a skill go in `7-certifications-and-training.md`; reference them from the skill entry rather than restating them.

**How to record it.** Grouped under `##` headings by category (adapt the categories to the candidate's field). Within a group, one line per skill:

`- **Skill** — depth: expert | working | familiar · last used: YYYY · evidence: role/project`

Depth means what it says: *expert* is "has designed and debugged this in production and could interview others on it", *working* is "has shipped with it unsupervised", *familiar* is "has touched it and could ramp". A skill with no evidence pointing at a role, project, degree or certification does not belong in the inventory — ask the candidate for the backing instead of recording it.
