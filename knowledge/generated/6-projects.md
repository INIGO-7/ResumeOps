# 6 — Projects

**What lives here.** Work done outside the scope of an employed role: side projects, open source contributions, portfolio pieces, hackathon entries, freelance or client work, research projects not tied to a degree, and substantial personal builds. Per project: what it does and for whom, why it was started, the stack, the candidate's own role if others were involved, the timeframe, its current state (live, archived, prototype), links to repo or demo, usage or traction if any, and what was technically hard about it.

**What does not live here.** Projects delivered as part of a job go in `3-work-experience.md`. A degree's thesis or final project goes in `2-academic-background.md`. Measurable outcomes from any project also get an entry in `4-achievement-bank.md`.

**How to record it.** One `###` heading per project formatted `Name — one-line what it is, YYYY–YYYY`. Under it, `- **Field:** value` lines for role, stack, status, links and traction, then a short paragraph on the problem and the hard part. Note explicitly whether the project is public and safe to put on a CV — client work is often not.
