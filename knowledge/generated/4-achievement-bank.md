# 4 — Achievement bank

**What lives here.** Every discrete accomplishment the candidate can defend, with its evidence. One entry per achievement: what the situation was, what the candidate personally did, and the measured outcome — percentages, latency, cost, revenue, hours saved, dataset size, users, team size, adoption. Where no number exists, the concrete consequence that followed instead. Each entry names the role, project or degree it came from and the raw source it was mined out of, so a claim on a CV can always be traced back.

This is the single most valuable file in the repo: CV bullets are drafted from here, not from `3-work-experience.md`.

**What does not live here.** Responsibilities and scope with no outcome attached — those stay in `3-work-experience.md`. Awards and third-party praise go in `9-awards-and-recognition.md`, though an award may also produce an achievement entry here.

**How to record it.** One `###` heading per achievement, a short imperative title. Under it:

- `**Where:** role or project, employer, year`
- `**What I did:** first-person, specific, the candidate's own contribution only`
- `**Outcome:** the number and what it is measured against, or the concrete consequence`
- `**Evidence:** the file in knowledge/raw/ or the conversation it came from`
- `**Confidence:** verified | candidate-stated | approximate`

Never round an approximate number into a precise-looking one, and never carry a metric forward that the candidate could not explain under questioning. Group entries under a `##` heading per employer or project when the file gets long.
